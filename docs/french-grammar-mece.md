# French grammar concepts — MECE reference

A complete, non-overlapping, exhaustive taxonomy of French grammar concepts organised by CEFR level (A1–C2). Every concept belongs to exactly one level. No concept appears twice.

---

## How to read this list

**MECE** stands for Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive — a structuring principle borrowed from consulting that ensures full coverage with zero overlap. Applied here:

- **Mutually exclusive** — each concept is assigned to the level where it is *first introduced*. It does not reappear at higher levels even though it continues to be used.
- **Collectively exhaustive** — together, all six levels cover every grammatical concept a learner will encounter from first contact through near-native mastery.

**Level assignment logic** — a concept is placed at the level where:
1. It is typically first taught in mainstream curricula (DELF/DALF, Alliance Française, CEFR guidelines)
2. A learner needs it to operate at that level with basic competence
3. It is not yet required for the level below

## A1 — Beginner

*First encounters with French. Goal: survive basic interactions, name things, describe the present.*

| Concept | Example | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Subject pronouns | je, tu, il, elle, nous, vous, ils, elles | Foundation of all conjugation |
| Être & avoir (present tense) | je suis, tu as | Two most essential verbs; treated as a unit at A1 |
| -er verb conjugation (present) | parler → je parle, tu parles | Regular -er is ~80% of common verbs |
| Indefinite articles | un, une, des | Introducing things for the first time |
| Definite articles | le, la, l', les | Referring to known or general things |
| Noun gender | le livre / la table | Binary masculine/feminine system |
| Basic adjective agreement | un petit chat / une petite chatte | Agreement in gender and number |
| Negation (ne…pas) | Je ne parle pas. | Core negative structure |
| Question formation | Tu parles ? / Est-ce que tu parles ? | Rising intonation and est-ce que |
| Numbers & quantities | un, deux, beaucoup de, peu de | Cardinal numbers + quantity expressions |
| Prepositions of place | dans, sur, sous, à, en, au | Spatial relationships |
| Possessive adjectives | mon, ma, mes, ton, ta, tes, son, sa, ses | Agreement with noun, not owner |
| C'est / il y a | C'est un livre. Il y a un chat. | Two essential presentational structures |
| Imperative (basic) | Écoute ! Regardez ! | Commands; 3 forms (tu, nous, vous) |

## A2 — Elementary

*Building basic fluency. Goal: describe past events, make plans, express preferences.*

| Concept | Example | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Passé composé with avoir | J'ai mangé une pomme. | Most common past tense; avoir auxiliary |
| Passé composé with être | Elle est allée au marché. | DR MRS VANDERTRAMP + reflexive verbs |
| Imparfait (introduction) | Il faisait beau. | Background description only at A2; contrast with PC is B1 |
| Futur proche | Je vais partir demain. | aller + infinitive; most common near-future form |
| -ir & -re verb conjugation (present) | finir, prendre | Second and third conjugation groups |
| Irregular verbs (core set) | aller, faire, pouvoir, vouloir, venir | High-frequency; must be memorised |
| Contracted articles | au, aux, du, des | à + le, de + le contractions |
| Partitive articles | du pain, de la crème, de l'eau | Expressing uncountable quantities |
| Adjective placement rules | une grande maison / un livre intéressant | Most adjectives follow; BAGS adjectives precede |
| Common adverbs | très, bien, souvent, toujours, jamais, déjà | Frequency and degree adverbs |
| Direct object pronouns | le, la, les → Je le vois. | Replace direct noun objects |
| Indirect object pronouns | lui, leur → Je lui parle. | Replace à + person |
| Demonstrative adjectives | ce, cet, cette, ces | Pointing to specific nouns |
| Reflexive verbs (present) | se lever, se coucher, s'appeler | Action performed on oneself |
| Comparative | plus grand que, aussi vite que, moins cher que | Three comparison structures |

## B1 — Intermediate

*Expressing ideas with nuance. Goal: handle most situations encountered while travelling; express opinions and feelings.*

| Concept | Example | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Imparfait vs passé composé (contrast) | Je dormais quand il est entré. | The key narrative tense distinction in French |
| Futur simple | Je partirai demain. | Formal future; distinct from futur proche |
| Conditionnel présent | Je voudrais un café. S'il venait... | Politeness, hypothesis, reported speech |
| Subjonctif présent (introduction) | Il faut que tu viennes. | Triggered by necessity, emotion, doubt |
| Relative pronouns | qui, que, où, dont | Linking clauses; dont is often problematic |
| Stressed (disjunctive) pronouns | moi, toi, lui, elle, nous, vous, eux, elles | After prepositions and for emphasis |
| Pronouns y and en | J'y vais. J'en veux deux. | y replaces à + place; en replaces de + thing/quantity |
| Double object pronouns | Il me le donne. | Order and placement rules |
| Superlative | le plus grand, la meilleure, le moins cher | Adjective and adverb superlatives |
| Extended negation | ne…plus, ne…jamais, ne…rien, ne…personne | Beyond basic ne…pas |
| Interrogative pronouns | Qui, que, quoi, lequel, laquelle | Direct and indirect question forms |
| Depuis / pendant / il y a | J'étudie depuis 2 ans. | Expressing duration and elapsed time |
| Causative faire | Je fais réparer la voiture. | faire + infinitive = have something done |
| Gérondif | en mangeant, en parlant | Simultaneous action; en + present participle |
| Si clauses (type 1 — open condition) | Si tu viens, on mangera ensemble. | Present + future; real/likely condition |

## B2 — Upper Intermediate

*Fluent and varied expression. Goal: interact with native speakers with spontaneity; produce clear, detailed text.*

| Concept | Example | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Subjonctif (extended triggers) | bien que, pour que, à moins que, avant que | Conjunctions requiring subjunctive |
| Plus-que-parfait | Il avait déjà mangé quand j'arrivai. | Past before another past |
| Futur antérieur | Quand tu auras fini, appelle-moi. | Future before another future |
| Conditionnel passé | J'aurais dû partir plus tôt. | Hypothetical past; regret |
| Passive voice | Le livre a été écrit par Camus. | être + past participle; agent with par |
| Si clauses (types 2 & 3 — hypothetical & counterfactual) | Si j'avais su, j'aurais agi autrement. | Imparfait + conditionnel; plus-que-parfait + conditionnel passé |
| Indirect speech (reported speech) | Il a dit qu'il viendrait. | Tense backshift rules |
| Nominalisation | partir → le départ, créer → la création | Converting verbs/adjectives into nouns |
| Complex relative pronouns | lequel, auquel, duquel, lesquels | After prepositions; agreement required |
| Concession & opposition connectors | pourtant, cependant, néanmoins, bien que | Linking contrasting ideas |
| Cause & consequence connectors | puisque, donc, c'est pourquoi, ainsi | Logical articulation of argument |
| Infinitive constructions | avant de partir, afin de réussir, sans savoir | Preposition + infinitive structures |
| Emphatic cleft structures | C'est lui qui a dit ça. C'est ça que je veux. | Topicalisation and focus |
| Register & levels of language | tu vs vous, manger vs se nourrir | Awareness of formal/informal registers |

## C1 — Advanced

*Sophisticated, precise expression. Goal: use language flexibly for social, academic, and professional purposes.*

| Concept | Example | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Subjonctif passé | Bien qu'il soit parti, je l'attends encore. | Past subjunctive in subordinate clauses |
| Subjonctif imparfait | Il fallait qu'il parlât. (literary) | Literary tense; rarely spoken, common in 19th–20th c. literature |
| Passé simple | Il alla, il vit, il vainquit. (literary/journalistic) | Narrative past in written French; replaces PC in formal prose |
| Passé antérieur | Quand il eut fini, il sortit. (literary) | Used with passé simple; past before past in formal narrative |
| Conditionnel journalistique | Le président aurait signé l'accord. | Unverified assertion; journalistic convention |
| Nuanced negation | ne…guère, ne…que, ne…point, ne…nul | Literary and precise negative forms |
| Abstract nominalisation | l'accroissement, la mise en place, le recours à | Complex noun phrases in academic/formal prose |
| Advanced discourse connectors | néanmoins, en revanche, quant à, par ailleurs | Formal argumentation and essay writing |
| Implicit subject participial phrases | Parti tôt, il arriva à l'heure. | Subject elision in formal writing |
| Complex passive constructions | se voir + infinitive, se faire + infinitive | Alternative passive structures |
| Stylistic inversion | Peut-être viendra-t-il. Ainsi parla-t-il. | Subject-verb inversion in formal and literary contexts |
| Idiomatic verb constructions | s'en aller, en vouloir à, se passer de, tenir à | Fixed collocations not deducible from components |

## C2 — Mastery

*Near-native precision and stylistic range. Goal: express with spontaneity, fluency, and precision in any situation.*

| Concept | Example | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Rare literary tenses | subjonctif plus-que-parfait (eût parlé) | Encountered in classical texts; almost never produced |
| Rhetorical structures | anaphore, chiasme, ellipse, zeugme | Figures of speech used for stylistic effect |
| Lexical precision | nuance between near-synonyms (peur / crainte / effroi) | Connotative and register differences |
| Regional & sociolectal variation | verlan, argot, Québécois French, Swiss French | Passive comprehension of non-standard registers |
| Archaic & literary vocabulary | iceux, ouïr, naguère, maint, sis | Found in classical literature and legal texts |
| Stylistic register switching | effortlessly moving between soutenu, courant, familier | Active mastery of all registers |
| Ellipsis & syntactic compression | Parti ! (= il est parti) / Possible. (= c'est possible) | Implicit structures in rapid speech and writing |
| Complex irony & implicature | understanding unsaid meaning in context | Pragmatic competence beyond grammar |

## Design notes

**Why some concepts appear later than expected**

The imparfait is introduced at A2 but the contrast between imparfait and passé composé is placed at B1. This is intentional — learners need to meet each tense separately before the distinction between them becomes the teachable point.

Similarly, the subjunctive is split: the basic triggers (il faut que, vouloir que) appear at B1, while the full range of conjunctions requiring it (bien que, à moins que, etc.) is B2. The concept is the same; the scope of application expands.

**Why C2 has fewer concepts**

C2 mastery is less about new grammatical structures and more about complete fluency within all structures already encountered. The C2 entries reflect areas where even near-fluent speakers have gaps: rare literary tenses they encounter in reading, register-switching ability, and pragmatic competence (understanding what is implied but not said). These are harder to drill than a conjugation table and require immersion as much as instruction.

**Concepts deliberately excluded**

The following are considered sub-components of listed concepts, not independent entries:

- Specific irregular verb tables (e.g. the full conjugation of *aller*) — these are instances of the "irregular verbs" concept, not separate concepts
- Individual conjunctions requiring the subjunctive — these are instances of "subjonctif extended triggers"
- Specific preposition + article contractions beyond au/du — variations within the contracted articles concept
- Phonetic rules (liaison, élision, enchaînement) — pronunciation system, not grammar system; outside scope
